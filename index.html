Succulents

Succulents stand out in the plant world due to their water-storing abilities, diverse appearance, adaptability, and ease of care, making them a popular choice for plant enthusiasts and gardeners alike.

Cactus

Specialty of cacti lies in their adaptations to arid environments, unique morphological features such as spines and reduced leaves, striking flowers, and their ability to thrive in a variety of conditions. Whether in their natural habitats or as decorative elements in homes, cacti are fascinating plants with a rich array of characteristics..

Vines and Creepers

Vines bring colour, texture and height into your garden. Their creeping and climbing habit makes them special because they can grow in ways that shrubs and trees simply cannot. For this reason, vines have great potential to become the foundation plantings in the garden, which tie it together, softening hard lines. Bonsai

Bonsai is an ancient living art form that utilizes growing and training techniques to produce miniature trees that mimic the appearance of their full-sized counterparts. These techniques include heavy crown pruning, root pruning, and root confinement in shallow containers

Fern

Japanese painted fern is a lot different from the plain green ferns that many gardeners are most familiar with, such as the interrupted fern (Osmunda claytoniana). True to its name, Japanese painted fern is more colorful, offering triangular, silvery leaves (called "fronds," technically) that sport an arching habit. As if that were not exciting enough, they also have grayish-green in them, as well as purplish midribs, making them variegated ferns. The fern is a slow-growing plant that adds about 12 inches to its overall size each year.
